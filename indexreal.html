Welcome to Child Safety in India

India is committed to ensuring the safety and well-being of its children through robust policies, laws, and dedicated departments. This website provides information on key organizations, legal frameworks, and resources to protect children from abuse, exploitation, and violence.

Key Child Safety Departments

Ministry of Women and Child Development

Oversees national policies and programs for child protection, including the Integrated Child Protection Scheme (ICPS).

National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR)

Monitors child rights violations and manages the SHe-Box complaint system for child sexual abuse cases.

CHILDLINE India Foundation

Operates the 1098 helpline, connecting children in need to support services across India.

Haryana State Child Protection Society (HSCPS)

Implements ICPS at the state level, providing institutional and non-institutional care for children.

Key Child Protection Laws

Juvenile Justice Act, 2015

Provides care and protection for children in need and addresses children in conflict with the law.

Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012

Protects children from sexual abuse and exploitation with stringent legal measures.

Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006

Prohibits child marriages and promotes awareness to end this practice.

Child Labour Act, 1986 (Amended 2016)

Regulates and prohibits child labor in hazardous occupations.
